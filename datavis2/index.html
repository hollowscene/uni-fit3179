The worst emitters

CO2 emissions are largely driven by richer countries (at least per capita) But in fact even worse than that is the small countries with tiny populations. See: New Caledonia (30.45 Tonnes per capita) Qatar (37.02 Tonnes per capita) Mongolia (26.98 Tonnes per capita) Trinidad and Tobago (25.37 Tonnes per capita) These are a special class of countries that have tiny populations but have absolutely massive CO2 contributions per capita due to large mining or oil based economies.
