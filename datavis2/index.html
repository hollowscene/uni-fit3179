THE WORST EMITTERS

CO2 emissions are largely driven by richer countries (at least per capita) But in fact even worse than these countries is a small subset of countries with tiny populations but massive carbon emissions such as: New Caledonia (30.45 Tonnes per capita) Qatar (37.02 Tonnes per capita) Mongolia (26.98 Tonnes per capita) Trinidad and Tobago (25.37 Tonnes per capita)
These are a distinct class of countries that have tiny populations but have absolutely massive CO2 contributions per capita due to large mining or oil based economies and reducing the carbon emissions in these countries should be a priority.
